# SALE ADVISOR — CLIENT PITCH GUIDE

---

## SECTION 1: THE ELEVATOR PITCH (30 SECONDS)

**The Go-To:**

"I run a company called Sale Advisor. Basically, we come to your house, go through everything you want to get rid of, and we sell it for you. We list it on every marketplace — Facebook Marketplace, eBay, Craigslist, all of them — and when it sells, we handle the delivery through our own moving company. You don't pay anything upfront. We just take a percentage when it sells. So you literally just point at stuff and we do the rest."

**The Shorter Version:**

"We sell people's stuff for them. You've got things sitting around your house you don't want? We come get it, list it everywhere, sell it, deliver it, and cut you a check. No cost to you unless it sells."

**The One-Liner (for networking or when someone asks at a bar):**

"You know all that stuff in your house you keep saying you're gonna sell? We actually do it for you."

---

## SECTION 2: THE WALKTHROUGH SCRIPT — THE IN-HOME CONSULTATION

### PHASE 1: THE ARRIVAL (First 2-3 Minutes)

The goal: make them comfortable. You are in their home. This is not a high-pressure sales call. This is two people walking through a house talking about stuff.

**At the door:**

"Hey! [Client name]? I'm [your name] with Sale Advisor. Thanks for having me over — appreciate you making time."

Read the room immediately. Are they chatty? Match that energy. Are they quiet and just want to get to it? Respect that.

**If they seem social:**
- Comment on something genuine — their dog, their yard, a piece of art.
- "Your place is great, by the way. Love the [specific thing]. Alright, so should we walk through everything?"

**If they seem all-business:**
- "Cool, well I don't want to take up too much of your time. Want to just walk me through what you're looking to sell and I'll take notes as we go?"

### PHASE 2: THE WALKTHROUGH (10-20 Minutes)

**How to start:**

"So the way this works is super simple — just show me everything you're thinking about selling. Doesn't matter how big or small. I'll take some photos, make some notes, and then I'll put together a game plan for you on what we think we can get and how fast things should move."

**As you assess items, say:**

For high-value items:
"Oh yeah, these move. [Brand/type] like this do really well on [specific platform]. We should be able to get you a solid number on this."

For mid-range items:
"This is a good one to bundle or list competitively. It'll move — just depends on the market that week."

For lower-value items:
"Honestly this one won't be a huge payday, but it's still worth listing. No reason to leave money on the table."

For items that genuinely won't sell:
"I'll be straight with you — this one's gonna be tough to move. We can still try, but I don't want to set you up expecting a big return. Worst case, we can donate it or toss it through our hauling company if you want it gone."

**Important:** Never make someone feel dumb for wanting to sell something. Even if it's a $15 item, treat it with respect.

### PHASE 3: SETTING EXPECTATIONS — PRICING AND TIMELINE (5 Minutes)

**The Pricing Conversation:**

"Alright so here's how the pricing works. I'm going to go back, do some research on everything we looked at — check comps, see what similar stuff has sold for recently — and then I'll send you over a breakdown. For each item, I'll give you a range of what I think we can realistically get. I always shoot for the high end, but I want to be honest with you upfront so there are no surprises."

**The Timeline Conversation:**

"Timeline-wise, some stuff sells in a day. Furniture in Chicago moves fast. Electronics, brand-name stuff — usually quick. Other things might sit for a week or two. I'll keep you posted on everything. You'll never have to wonder what's going on."

### PHASE 4: THE "IS THIS EVEN WORTH IT?" MOMENT

Almost every client will have this moment.

**Your response:**

"Listen, you'd be surprised. I've walked into houses where people thought they had nothing and we ended up pulling two, three grand out of stuff they were about to throw away. That's literally why we exist — most people have no idea what their stuff is actually worth. That's our job to figure out. And even if some of it's only worth twenty bucks, that's still twenty bucks you didn't have. It adds up."

"And honestly, the worst case scenario is we get rid of stuff you don't want anymore. Best case, you make real money off it. Either way you win."

### PHASE 5: THE CLOSE — NEXT STEPS (2-3 Minutes)

Do not make this weird. This should feel like the natural end of a conversation.

**What to say:**

"Alright, so here's what happens next. I'm going to go back, put together your full breakdown — every item, what I think we can get, and which platforms I'd list each one on. I'll send that over to you [today/tomorrow/within 48 hours]. You look it over, and if everything looks good, we get started."

"Once you give us the green light, we'll come back, pick everything up, and start listing. From there, you just sit back. When stuff sells, we handle delivery through our moving company and you get paid. That's it."

"Any questions for me?"

**Before you leave:**

"Appreciate you letting me come through. I'll get that breakdown over to you [timeframe]. And if you think of anything else you want to add to the list, just shoot me a text."

## SECTION 3: TOP 10 OBJECTIONS AND EXACTLY WHAT TO SAY

### 1. "What's your cut?"

"We take [X]% of the sale price. That covers everything — the listing, the photos, the communication with buyers, the delivery, all of it. You don't pay a dollar unless we actually sell your stuff. So we're motivated to get you the best price possible because that's how we make more too. We're on the same team here."

### 2. "I could just do this myself."

"You absolutely could. But here's what usually happens — you list a couple things, you get a bunch of 'is this still available?' messages from people who never respond, you spend a weekend going back and forth, and then the stuff just sits there. We do this every single day. We know how to price it, where to list it, and how to get it in front of the right buyers. Plus, we handle the delivery."

### 3. "What if my stuff doesn't sell?"

"Then you don't pay us anything. That's the whole model — no upfront cost, no risk to you. If it doesn't sell, we'll adjust the pricing, try different platforms, whatever we need to do."

### 4. "How do I know I can trust you?"

"We come from Lakeshore Hauling — we've been running a moving and junk removal company for about four years now across multiple cities. Five-star reviews across the board. I can send you our reviews, our business info, whatever you need to feel comfortable. And everything goes through a written agreement. Full transparency."

### 5. "My stuff isn't worth much."

"You'd be surprised how fast it all adds up. That end table you think is worth ten bucks? Maybe it's thirty. Those old golf clubs? Could be a hundred and fifty. We've done walkthroughs where clients thought they had a few hundred dollars and it ended up being well over a thousand."

### 6. "I'm not ready to sell yet / I need to think about it."

"Totally fine. No pressure at all. I'll send you the breakdown anyway so you have it whenever you're ready. The only thing I'd mention is — the sooner you list, the sooner you get paid. A patio set in May is worth a lot more than a patio set in November."

### 7. "Can I just sell a few things and keep the rest?"

"A hundred percent. This isn't all-or-nothing. You tell me exactly what you want us to take, and that's what we take. And if later you decide you want to add more, just hit me up."

### 8. "What if I change my mind after you've listed something?"

"No problem. If it hasn't sold yet, we pull the listing. It's your stuff until someone buys it. Just give us a heads up and we'll take it down."

### 9. "I've had bad experiences selling online / had scammers."

"Yeah, and that's exactly why we exist. We vet buyers, we handle all the communication, and we handle delivery through our own moving company. You never have to meet a stranger, you never have to load anything into your car, and you never have to deal with any of the back-and-forth."

### 10. "Do you handle big items? / How does delivery work?"

"That's actually one of our biggest advantages. We own a moving company — Lakeshore Hauling. So when something sells, we deliver it ourselves. Couches, dressers, dining tables, you name it. Most people listing on Marketplace have to figure out delivery on their own, and a lot of sales fall through because of it. With us, that's built in."

## SECTION 4: THE FOLLOW-UP FRAMEWORK

### IMMEDIATELY AFTER THE VISIT (Within 2 Hours)

**Text:**
"Hey [name]! It was great meeting you today. I'm working on your breakdown now — I'll have it over to you by [specific time/day]. If you think of anything else you want to add to the list, just let me know. Talk soon!"

### THE BREAKDOWN (Within 24-48 Hours)

Send the full item breakdown with:
- Every item listed out
- Estimated sale price (range if needed)
- Which platform(s) for each item
- Total estimated payout after percentage
- Simple summary of next steps

### IF NO RESPONSE — FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

- **Day 3:** "Hey [name]! Just wanted to make sure you got the breakdown I sent over. Let me know if you have any questions."
- **Day 7:** "Hey [name], just checking in one more time. No pressure — whenever you're ready, we're here."
- **Day 14:** "Hey [name]! Last check-in from me. If you ever decide you want to move forward, you've got my number. Hope you're doing well!"

Then stop. Nobody likes being hounded.

### AFTER THE SALE

"Hey [name]! Just wanted to let you know everything has sold and your payout is [amount]. It was awesome working with you. If you ever have more stuff or know anyone who does — send them my way!"

This is where referrals happen. Happy clients who just got a check with zero effort are the most likely people on earth to tell their friends.

---

## FINAL NOTES FOR THE TEAM

1. You are in someone's home. Be respectful. Take your shoes off if they do.
2. Never make someone feel bad about what they're selling. A $20 lamp gets the same energy as a $2,000 couch.
3. Be honest about pricing. Overpromising kills trust and kills referrals.
4. Read the room. Some clients want to chat. Others want you in and out. Respect that.
5. Follow up when you say you will. Reliability is the bare minimum.
6. Every interaction is a commercial for Sale Advisor. Five-star service is not optional.
7. The best close is not a close at all. It's a conversation that ends with "yeah, let's do it."
